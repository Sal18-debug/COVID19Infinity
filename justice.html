Civil Rights and Social Justice

Social movements and new technologies can be combined to further support activist movements. Such a combination can tackle legal, social, and moral issues in social justice even during this pandemic. Examples of such are shown here.

CRSJ COVID-19 Series

The American Bar Association has a Civil Rights and Social Justice section, which has developed a multi-part webinar series exploring how the COVID-19 pandemic highlights critical legal issues of criminal justice, civil rights, human rights, and economic concerns, to name only a few. Visit their website to learn about their free webinars where panelists will address the deepening crises in our collective pursuit of advancing law and justice.

Technology And Social Justice

Tal Frankfurt, Founder and CEO of Cloud for Good, writes about how technology is fueling social justice during the time of the coronavirus.

Rethinking Social Change in the Face of Coronavirus

Stanford is offering a new series of articles that delve into many aspects of life that have been altered by the arrival of Coronavirus, from individual relationships to institutional operations to international collaborations. Visit this website to gain insight from social change leaders around the globe to help organizations face the systemic, operational, and strategic challenges that will test the limits of their capabilities.
